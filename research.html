The Experimental Hypothesis

A hypothesis is a proposed prediction that needs to be tested. The aim of an experiment is to test whether the hypothesis is supported by the behavior of people.

Drinking one cup of coffee per day (as opposed to zero cups) will increase self-reported anxiety in college students.

The Null Hypothesis

In order to conduct a valid experiment, it must be possible for the predicted effects to occur or not to occur. The hypothesis must always be tested against a null hypothesis, stating that the expected results will not occur.

Drinking one cup of coffee per day (as opposed to zero cups) will not increase self-reported anxiety in college students.

Variables

The variable manipulated by the researcher is known as the independent variable. The second variable, representing the participant's scores, is known as the dependent variable. This is because the scores are dependent on how the researcher manipulates the independent variable.

  • The independent variable is whether the participant has consumed one or zero cups of coffee.

  • The dependent variable is the participant's self-reported anxiety on a 10-point scale.

A basic experimental design is to allocate people to experimental conditions representing different conditions for the independent variable.

  • Condition A - Consumed one cup of coffee.

  • Condition B - Consumed zero cups of coffee.
